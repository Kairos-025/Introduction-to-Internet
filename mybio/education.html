My Education Background

Education

My academic pursuits have been centred around a Bachelor's degree in Computer Science at the University of Windsor. This educational venture serves as the foundation upon which I build my understanding of the theoretical aspects of computing. Through lectures, practical sessions, and projects, I have gained insights into the diverse facets of this dynamic field. I have delved into a comprehensive curriculum encompassing various foundational and specialized courses.

In the early stages, courses such as "Introduction to Algorithms and Programming" laid the groundwork for my understanding of fundamental programming concepts. This course honed my coding skills and instilled a problem-solving mindset essential in computer science.

"Computer Architecture" provided insights into the design and organization of computer systems, offering a deeper understanding of the hardware that underlies software functionality. Meanwhile, "Introduction to the Internet" broadened my perspective on the interconnected world of online communication and information exchange.

"Computer Concepts for End-Users" facilitated a user-centric approach, teaching me to design and implement systems with the end-user in mind. This course emphasized the importance of creating technology that is accessible and user-friendly.

In mathematics, "Calculus" enriched my analytical abilities, providing a mathematical foundation crucial for tackling complex problems in computer science. "Key Concepts in Computer Science" also served as a bridge between theoretical concepts and practical applications, offering a holistic view of the core principles shaping the field.
